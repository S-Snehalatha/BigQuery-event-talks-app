Major News Headlines for June 16, 2026:

1. US-Iran Deal to End War
- Agreement Details: Following weeks of negotiations, the U.S. and Iran have announced a deal to end their conflict, with a formal signing ceremony scheduled for Friday, June 19, in Geneva.
- Strait of Hormuz: U.S. President Donald Trump stated that the Strait of Hormuz is expected to be "completely open" and toll-free by Friday. However, Mitsui OSK Lines notes that it may take weeks for shipping lines to resume transit, awaiting evidence that the agreement is secure and material.
- Nuclear Provisions: U.S. Vice President JD Vance confirmed that Iran has agreed to eliminate its enriched uranium stockpile as part of the framework to ensure the country never obtains a nuclear weapon.
- Political Tensions: Friction remains, as Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu acknowledged a fallout with President Trump over the agreement.

2. Global News
- California Plane Crash: A U.S. Air Force B-52 Stratofortress bomber crashed during a routine test flight from Edwards Air Force Base in California. All eight people on board are believed to have perished.
- UK Social Media Ban: British Prime Minister Keir Starmer announced that the UK will implement a total ban on access to major social media apps for anyone under the age of 16.
- EU Sanctions on Russia: The European Union has adopted a new package of sanctions targeting 34 individuals and 47 entities, focusing on Russia's "shadow fleet," military-industrial complex, and energy revenues.

3. India News

- Legal Developments: A Delhi court rejected a bail plea from former Reliance Communications director Punit Narendra Garg in connection with a money laundering case. An Amritsar court granted anticipatory bail to Shiromani Akali Dal's Bikram Singh Majithia.
